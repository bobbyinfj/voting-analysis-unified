What is King County Proposition 1?

King County Proposition 1, approved by voters on April 22, 2025, authorized a seven-year property tax levy beginning in 2026 to fund the Regional Automated Fingerprint Identification System (AFIS).

Purpose

Fund the Regional AFIS, a forensic tool used across all cities and unincorporated areas of King County to store and match fingerprint records for crime-solving purposes.

Tax Rate

The levy imposes a tax rate of 2.75 cents per $1,000 of assessed property value. For a median-priced home, this equates to approximately $15-20 per year.

Duration

Seven-year property tax levy beginning in 2026, running through 2032.

Election Result

Approved with 59.72% of voters in favor (151,495 votes in favor, 102,189 against).

Historical Context: Ballot Measures & Senate Joint Resolutions

Understanding Washington State Ballot Measures

Washington State uses several types of ballot measures, each with different origins and purposes:

üìã Senate Joint Resolutions (SJR)

Senate Joint Resolutions are constitutional amendments proposed by the state legislature. They require:

  • Two-thirds majority approval in both House and Senate
  • Approval by voters in a general election
  • Used for changes to the state constitution

Examples: SJR 8201 (WA Cares Fund investment), SJR 8200 (various constitutional amendments)

üìù Initiatives

Initiatives are citizen-proposed measures that:

  • Are placed on the ballot by voter petition
  • Require signatures from registered voters
  • Can propose laws or constitutional amendments
  • Bypass the legislature entirely

Examples: Various tax measures, environmental regulations, healthcare reforms

History of Senate Joint Resolutions in Washington

Senate Joint Resolutions have been used throughout Washington's history to amend the state constitution on important policy matters:

  • Early 20th Century: SJRs established fundamental rights and government structures
  • Mid-20th Century: SJRs addressed taxation, education funding, and environmental protections
  • Late 20th Century: SJRs focused on term limits, campaign finance, and judicial reforms
  • 21st Century: SJRs have addressed healthcare, long-term care, and social safety net programs

Key Context: Senate Joint Resolutions represent major policy decisions that require both legislative consensus and voter approval, making them significant democratic processes in Washington State governance.

Recent Senate Joint Resolutions (2020s)

Recent SJRs have addressed critical statewide issues:

  • SJR 8201: Proposed constitutional amendment allowing WA Cares Fund (long-term care insurance) to invest in stocks and equities
  • SJR 8200: Various constitutional amendments proposed by the legislature
  • Previous SJRs: Have addressed voting rights, taxation authority, and healthcare funding

These measures reflect ongoing debates about fiscal policy, social programs, and the role of state government in Washington.

King County Proposition 1 in Context

King County Proposition 1 differs from Senate Joint Resolutions in important ways:

  • Local vs. State: Prop 1 is a county-level measure, not a constitutional amendment
  • Property Tax Levy: It's a specific funding mechanism for county services
  • Regional Impact: While county-wide, it affects all municipalities within King County
  • Continuation: This measure renews existing funding, demonstrating ongoing voter support for AFIS

Understanding this distinction helps voters recognize the difference between statewide constitutional changes (SJRs) and local funding measures (like Prop 1).

Understanding AFIS

What is the Automated Fingerprint Identification System?

The Regional Automated Fingerprint Identification System (AFIS) is a critical forensic tool that:

  • ‚úì Stores fingerprint records from across King County
  • ‚úì Matches fingerprints found at crime scenes to known individuals
  • ‚úì Serves all 39 cities and unincorporated areas in King County
  • ‚úì Is used by law enforcement agencies throughout the region
  • ‚úì Has been instrumental in solving thousands of crimes

üîç How AFIS Works

When law enforcement collects fingerprints from a crime scene, AFIS compares them against a database of millions of fingerprint records. This rapid matching helps identify suspects and solve cases more efficiently.

üí∞ Cost to Taxpayers

For a home valued at $700,000 (approximately median home value in King County), the annual cost is about $19.25 per year. This equates to less than $0.05 per day.
