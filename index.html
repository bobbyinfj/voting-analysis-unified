Official Sources

Quick Bite

Congressional Budget Office (CBO) ‚Äî Non-partisan fiscal analysis of federal legislation.

Snack

The CBO provides objective, non-partisan analysis of budgetary and economic issues. When Congress considers major legislation, the CBO analyzes its fiscal impact, cost estimates, and economic effects. Their reports are widely trusted by both parties and essential for understanding the financial implications of federal policies.

Meal

The Congressional Budget Office (CBO) is a non-partisan federal agency that provides Congress with objective, timely analysis of budgetary and economic issues. Established in 1974, the CBO produces:

  • Cost Estimates: Projected budgetary effects of proposed legislation
  • Baseline Projections: 10-year economic and budget forecasts
  • Analysis Reports: Deep dives into policy options and their fiscal impacts
  • Working Papers: Technical research on economic and budgetary topics

CBO analyses are particularly valuable for understanding the financial implications of federal ballot measures, tax proposals, and major policy changes. The office's non-partisan reputation makes it a trusted source for voters evaluating fiscal policy.

Quick Bite

Congress.gov ‚Äî Official source for federal legislation, voting records, and congressional activity.

Snack

Congress.gov is the official website for U.S. federal legislative information. It provides access to bills, resolutions, congressional records, committee reports, and voting records. This is the primary source for tracking what Congress is doing and how your representatives vote.

Meal

Congress.gov is the official website for U.S. federal legislative information, maintained by the Library of Congress. It's the most authoritative source for:

  • Bills & Resolutions: Full text, status, amendments, and voting history
  • Congressional Record: Daily proceedings and debates
  • Committee Reports: Detailed analysis from congressional committees
  • Voting Records: How each member voted on every bill
  • Member Profiles: Contact information, committee assignments, sponsored bills

For voters, Congress.gov is invaluable for:

  • Tracking how your representatives vote on key issues
  • Reading the actual text of proposed federal legislation
  • Understanding the legislative process and timeline
  • Finding primary source documents without interpretation

This raw data is essential for making informed voting decisions, especially when evaluating candidates' records and understanding what federal ballot measures actually do vs. how they're presented in campaigns.

Colorado

Quick Bite

Coloradoan ‚Äî Local Fort Collins newspaper with objective ballot measure analysis.

Snack

The Coloradoan is a USA Today affiliate covering Fort Collins and Larimer County. Their election coverage includes detailed, objective analyses of local ballot measures and candidates. The reporting is generally balanced and provides context for how measures might affect residents.

Meal

The Coloradoan (Fort Collins Coloradoan) is the primary local news source for Larimer County, Colorado, and is part of the USA Today Network. For election coverage, they provide:

  • Ballot Measure Analysis: Detailed explanations of what measures do, their costs, and potential impacts
  • Candidate Profiles: Background information and positions on key issues
  • Election Results: Real-time and comprehensive post-election coverage
  • Voter Guides: Consolidated information about all items on local ballots

The Coloradoan's analyses are particularly valuable because they:

  • Focus on local impact and practical effects on residents
  • Explain complex ballot language in accessible terms
  • Provide context about local political dynamics
  • Include quotes and perspectives from both supporters and opponents

As a USA Today affiliate, the Coloradoan maintains journalistic standards while providing the local context that national outlets can't. Their election coverage helps voters understand how ballot measures will specifically affect Fort Collins and Larimer County.
